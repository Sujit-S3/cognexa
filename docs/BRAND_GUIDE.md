# Cognexa Brand Guide

## Brand overview

**Cognexa** is a premium AI-powered learning management system for modern educational institutions, instructors, enterprises, and learners.

**Tagline:** Connecting Knowledge, Empowering Minds.

The name combines cognition with connection. It signals a platform where people, content, insight, and intelligent assistance work together. The brand should feel modern, premium, intelligent, minimal, trustworthy, innovative, academic, and future-ready.

### Mission

Make high-quality knowledge easier to teach, discover, and master.

### Vision

A world where every mind can reach the right knowledge at the right moment.

### Core values

1. **Human potential first.** Technology amplifies the judgement, creativity, and care of learners and educators.
2. **Clarity builds trust.** Every interaction should be understandable, accessible, and worthy of important work.
3. **Progress with purpose.** Success means durable knowledge, confident teaching, and meaningful growth.
4. **Intelligence with responsibility.** AI must be secure, transparent, bounded, and accountable to people.
5. **Connection creates value.** Learning improves when people, context, and feedback move together.

## Logo system

The primary identity combines the Cognexa wordmark with a rounded-square **C** connection mark. Use the full lockup in navigation, authentication, marketing, and institutional surfaces. Use the mark alone for favicons, compact navigation, avatars, and application icons.

- Keep clear space around the logo equal to at least half the mark width.
- Never stretch, rotate, outline, recolor individual elements, or place the mark on a visually noisy background.
- Prefer the full-color mark on `#020617` or `#F8FAFC`.
- Use a single-color white or near-black treatment only when reproduction requires it.
- Do not combine the logo with an alternate tagline or a third-party mark without a documented partnership lockup.

## Color palette

| Role                 | Hex       | Use                                                       |
| -------------------- | --------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| Primary indigo       | `#4F46E5` | Primary actions, focus, active navigation, brand emphasis |
| Secondary cyan       | `#06B6D4` | Connected states, data highlights, secondary emphasis     |
| Accent violet        | `#8B5CF6` | AI features, gradients, selected premium moments          |
| Light background     | `#F8FAFC` | Light-theme canvas                                        |
| Dark background      | `#020617` | Primary dark-theme canvas                                 |
| Primary light text   | `#F8FAFC` | High-emphasis text on dark surfaces                       |
| Secondary light text | `#CBD5E1` | Supporting text on dark surfaces                          |

Indigo is the dominant action color. Cyan communicates connection and live intelligence. Violet is an accent, not a default fill. Gradients should be restrained and should never reduce text contrast.

## Typography

- **Headings:** Space Grotesk, weights 500–700. Use tight but readable tracking.
- **Body and interface:** Inter, weights 400–700.
- **Code and technical data:** JetBrains Mono where monospacing improves comprehension.

Use sentence case for headings and controls. Avoid all caps except short labels, status badges, or verified credential treatments. Maintain comfortable line lengths of roughly 45–80 characters for body content.

## Spacing and composition

Use a 4 px base grid with 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96, and 128 px as the primary rhythm. Layouts should be spacious, focused, and calm. Prefer one clear primary action per surface and progressive disclosure over dense control panels.

Glass surfaces may support hierarchy but must retain readable borders, sufficient opacity, and high contrast. Decorative auroras should never compete with learning content.

## Iconography

Use one consistent outlined icon family with rounded geometry and a 1.5–2 px stroke. Icons support labels; they do not replace labels for unfamiliar actions. Reserve filled icons for selected states and urgent status. Avoid novelty emoji in core institutional workflows.

## Voice and tone

Cognexa sounds clear, capable, calm, encouraging, and informed.

- Write directly to the learner or educator.
- Prefer precise outcomes over hype and unsupported superlatives.
- Explain AI behaviour and limitations plainly.
- Use “you” and active voice; avoid jargon when ordinary language is clearer.
- Celebrate progress without infantilising professional or academic users.
- Error messages state what happened, what remains safe, and the next useful action.

Example: “Your draft is saved. Reconnect to publish this lesson.”  
Avoid: “Oops! Our magical AI had a hiccup!”

## UI principles

1. **Learning is the focal point.** Navigation and decoration should recede behind the current learning goal.
2. **One system, distinct roles.** Learner, instructor, and administrator workspaces share patterns while exposing only role-relevant actions.
3. **Visible system state.** Saving, processing, publishing, assessment, and AI generation always show status and recovery paths.
4. **Responsible intelligence.** AI output is identifiable, reviewable, and never presented as unquestionable authority.
5. **Motion with meaning.** Animation explains change or hierarchy, stays GPU-friendly, and respects reduced-motion preferences.

## Accessibility

- Meet WCAG 2.2 AA contrast and interaction requirements.
- Preserve visible focus, semantic landmarks, logical heading order, and complete keyboard access.
- Never encode meaning through color alone.
- Provide text alternatives for informative media and captions/transcripts for time-based learning content.
- Support 200% text zoom and layouts down to 320 px without loss of function.
- Motion must respect `prefers-reduced-motion`; autoplaying or looping effects need a non-motion fallback.
